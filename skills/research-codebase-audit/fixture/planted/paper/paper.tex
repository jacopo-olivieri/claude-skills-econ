\begin{abstract}
We estimate the effect of rainfall shocks on household income using a three-village
panel. A one-standard-deviation rainfall shock reduces log household income by
8.3 percent.
\end{abstract}

\section{Data}

We use a household panel covering three villages over two survey waves. Income is measured
in thousands of local currency units.
Total household income is the sum of the four components recorded in the survey instrument:
crop sales, livestock sales, wage earnings, and remittances.
The estimation sample excludes households observed in fewer than two survey waves, leaving
a balanced panel of 4,832 household-year observations (2,416 households across the two waves).
Of these, 725 households --- 25 percent of the panel --- experienced a negative rainfall shock
in at least one wave.
Estimates are based on the public one-in-ten subsample of the survey; the complete enumeration
is held under restricted access and is not distributed with this package.
% The raw file also contains enumerator scratch codes that we discard.

Rainfall is recorded at the village level. The shock variable is the standardised deviation
of wave rainfall from the village's long-run historical mean (the 1991--2020 climate
normal).

\section{Empirical strategy}

We regress log household income on the rainfall shock and household controls. All
regressions are weighted using the survey weights provided with the sampling frame, and
standard errors are clustered at the village level. To limit the influence of outliers,
wage earnings (\texttt{wage\_earnings}) are winsorised at the 99th percentile before
entering total income.

\section{Results}

Table~\ref{tab:main} reports the main estimate. A one-standard-deviation rainfall shock
reduces log household income by 0.083, statistically significant at the five percent
level.

\appendix

\section{Rainfall data construction}
\label{app:rain}

The village rainfall series used to construct the shock is built in three steps:
\begin{enumerate}
\item daily rain-gauge records are aggregated to survey-wave totals;
\item each village is matched to every rain gauge within a 15-km radius of its centroid;
\item village rainfall is the mean of the matched gauges' wave totals.
\end{enumerate}
The gauge match was performed at the national meteorological service, and gauge coordinates
are not distributed with this package. The resulting village-level series ships with the
package and is merged into the household file.
