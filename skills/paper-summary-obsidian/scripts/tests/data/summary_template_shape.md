1. Research Question and Motivation
#### Question
- Does rural road access raise household consumption? [p. 1, Section 1]
#### Why it matters
- Roads absorb a large share of development budgets [p. 2]
2. Data and Methods
### 2.1 Data (source, type, time span)
- Household survey, 2010-2015, 500 villages [p. 4, Section 2.1]
### 2.2 Method
- Regression discontinuity at the population threshold [p. 5, Section 2.2]
3. Results
- Consumption rises 8% (se 2%) [p. 8, Table 3]
- No effect on migration [p. 9]
4. Limitations and Extensions
### 4.1 Limitations
- Author-stated: short five-year panel [p. 12]
### 4.2 Concrete directions for future research
- Extend to labour-market outcomes [p. 13]
5. Synthesis of findings
Roads raise consumption but not migration.
Effects concentrated in poorer villages.
