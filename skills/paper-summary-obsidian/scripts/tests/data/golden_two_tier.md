## Reading notes

%% begin annotations %%

### 💬 Research Question and Motivation %% fold %%

- Rural roads are hypothesised to raise consumption by lowering trade costs.
  - The paper asks whether new all-weather roads change household consumption [p. 1, Section 1].
  - Roads absorb a large share of development budgets, so the return matters for policy [p. 2].

### 📌 Data and Empirical Strategy %% fold %%

- Identification rests on a population-threshold discontinuity in road eligibility.
  - Household survey covers 2010-2015 across 500 villages [p. 4, Section 2.1].
  - Regression discontinuity at the threshold governing eligibility [p. 5, Section 2.2].

### 🎯 Results %% fold %%

- Road access raises consumption without inducing migration.
  - Consumption rises 8% (se 2%) [p. 8, Table 3].
  - Migration is unchanged [p. 9].

### ✒️ Limitations and Extensions %% fold %%

- The short panel limits long-run inference.
  - Author-stated: only five years of data [p. 12].
